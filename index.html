Welcome to LX Legal & Tax

The ‘go to’ law firm in Mauritius

LX Legal & Tax is a business law firm advising clients on their transactions, investments, tax advisory and disputes. The firm is the ‘go to’ law firm for sophisticated clients who demand high quality, responsive lawyers and business consultants. We are a boutique law firm with a long standing and in-depth international experience. Our clients are assured that their interests are represented in the most efficient, confidential, effective and business-like manner. We are aware and conversant with the expectations of the corporate and cross border matters. We are geared up to meet those exigencies.

Our mission is to provide clients with the highest quality legal services. Clients deal directly with senior lawyers and consultants. Our team means business and is committed to your interests so that you may be successful. We accompany clients to fulfil their objectives as valued partners.

We offer more than just legal skills
We consider and service clients as partners with whom we share valuable connections in the banking, regulatory, financial advisory and stakeholder relations.

We ensure that unnecessary risks do not burden your business objectives. In case of litigation and exposure to reputational risk we consider that an early risk assessment and risk avoidance measures are of paramount importance.

At LX more than just being a good lawyer or a good counsel we walk the extra mile to provide practical, business-oriented, and value-added legal services. We want to “mean for your business” and to be the successful “X factor” in your ventures.

Gilbert is a senior partner at LX Legal & Tax. He has more than 25 years of experience as a financial consultant and lawyer. He generally practices in the field of company, corporate financial services law and also in commercial litigation. Gilbert is qualified as an attorney in the Republic of Mauritius.

Gilbert provides legal counsel to local and international companies including international Banks, Investment Funds, Global Petrochemical Company, African Telecommunication Organization and Real Estate Companies. Gilbert’s versatility has allowed him to represent clients in some of the most complex transactions and commercial disputes before the Supreme Court of Mauritius. He is defined by the IFLR as “a highly regarded and leading lawyer in banking, disputes, insolvency, M&A, restructuring” and is described as an “excellent, very pragmatic and responsive” professional by the Chambers Global.

He has worked for reputed institutions such as Merrill Lynch (USA), Multi-consult (part of the Arthur Andersen Network), Ernst & Young Financial Services and Appleby during his career. He also practiced as an attorney until 2003 when he was the adviser to the Minister of Financial Services and Corporate Affairs. He was involved in the preparation and drafting of relevant legislation. He resumed private practice at the beginning of 2004.

Gilbert has assisted Macquarie Bank in respect of a Real Estate Investment Trusts for investments in China amounting to more than USD 1.5 billion. He also advised Shell in the Royal Dutch Shell Plc sale on its USD 1 billion majority in African Businesses to Vitol Group. He represented Rascom with respect to a facility of about USD 111 million as regards the financing of a second satellite for the African Development Bank. Gilbert also successfully defended Citic Capital in claim for USD 50 million before the Supreme Court of Mauritius which was in relation to a guarantee agreement, receivership and corporate actions.

Gilbert is the founder member of the International Fiscal Association (Mauritius chapter), member of Insol and was a council member of the Mauritius Law Society. He also sits on the board of the Mauritius Law Reform Commission whereby he chairs the advisory committee on financial services. He additionally acts as a lecturer on Company Law to prospective attorneys, barristers and notaries at the Council of Vocational and Legal Education.

Why Maurtius

A young country, which only gained independence in 1968, Mauritius is ranked among the first in Africa for its ease of doing business, for economic stability and good governance. Mauritius has evolved steadily and sustainably from a mono-crop sugar economy to a well diversified economy which its main activities ranging from seafood export, textile,tourism, construction, telecommunication, to business and financial services.

Mauritius is recognized as a unique investment destination which provide to foreign investors simple tax procedures and investment laws. The island has attracted US$10.98 billion in foreign direct investment inflows and has encouraged more than 9,000 offshore entities willing to trade in India and in South Africa.

With a unique blend of cultures and ethnicities, Mauritius is a multilingual country. English is the official language of the island, but mauritians are also fluent in French, Mauritian Creole, and several Asian languages – including Hindi, Bhojpuri, Urdu, and Chinese

Reasons to invest in Mauritius

  • A political, social and economic stability
  • A solid reputation as a secure and reliable investment hub
  • An excellence business, telecommunication and IT infrastructures
  • A diversified economy
  • A multilingual workforce
  • An availability of qualified workforce with varied competencies
  • A business friendly environment
  • A preferential access to international markets
  • A comprehensive and forward looking legal framework
  • A safe and excellent living environment

Reasons to do business in Mauritius

  • A corporate and income tax of 15%
  • Global Business Companies Category 2 are tax-exempt
  • Global Business Companies Category 1 has maximum effective tax rate of 3%
  • Tax free dividends
  • No capital gains tax
  • Convenient time zone
  • Low cost administration, staffs and equipments.
  • Up to 100% foreign ownership
  • Foreign Trade Zones / Free Ports
  • Free repatriation of profits, dividends and capital
  • No minimum foreign capital required
  • Double Tax treaties
  • No exchange control
